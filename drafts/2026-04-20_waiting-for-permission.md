Waiting for Permission

   So many times, I  have said to myself something like "I wish I could
 take a rest for a few minutes; just lie down with  toes up.' Or "What a
beautiful day. The sky is so blue. I wish I had time to take a walk."
    Or "I wonder  how my old  college roommate  is doing. Maybe I'll give
her a call. It has been so long."And then I don't act on it. Does this
resonate with you?    Setting aside thoughts  of  wishes and wants, while
 knowing that  they are just what we need  for the health of our  body, our
mind and soul?  This intuitive knowing is   part  of  the wisdom  that
comes with old age. It is a precious gift. But knowing is different from
doing. And too  often we back away the wishing and wanting and  wait  for
permission to act.

  When  we were children, we  needed to get  permission from our parents to
do things  we wanted, and permission was  often only granted  after  we
completed a chore.    And later, always,  "homework comes first." Over time
we grew into adulthood, "with all the right and privileges pertaining
thereto."  And in response we fiercely juggled  parallel   responsibilities
 of raising  a family and rising a career. For  many years, we  did not
have the luxury of  "free  time,"   and   put  " wishes and wants" on hold
for "someday" when time would be available.

   Old age came, and along with it came  the opportunity for uncommitted
time. But it came along  with a diminution of energy and the emergence of
new demands  as we coped with age-related changes and needs vying for  our
time. We needed to apportion it mindfully.  Our wisdom helped  us. Being an
older person  comes along with knowing what  is good for us and supports
us.    And we know that that waiting for anything, including permission,
wastes time, an  increasingly limited commodity the older we get.

   Old age is not futureless nor  passionless. We older persons  have
wishes and dreams and creative ideas .We have reached the "someday, "and
our enhanced wisdom enables us to recognize we are the one to grant the
permission we have been waiting for. We have finally reached the age of
agency!  While we a contuke to be responsible for our body, mind and soul,
we  can give ourselves permission to do so.

We can  get an ice cream, call a college roommate, make new friends, take a
walk, enroll in a course, learn something new,  and rest when we need to.
We can also give ourselves  permission to *not do* what we know  is not
good for us. We can let go of things, activities and relationships that no
longer support us .

   Be bold and say it out loud: *I give myself permission to…*  Because the
 permission  comes from you, and  you are a wise person who knows that.
